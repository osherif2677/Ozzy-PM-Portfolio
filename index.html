Amazon Fresh · Assistant Manager

Inventory Health & Shrink Program

Jan 2025 – Ongoing · Center Store, Kitchen, Paleo, Outbound, Front End / CX
  • Problem: Low-80s inventory execution and shrink near 19% of shipped OPS.
  • Mechanism: Built an Excel control sheet tracking HP Agile, culling, expiration, gaps, and shrink by quarter.
  • Execution: Weekly huddles with department leads; each owner acted on a short list of flagged items / locations.
Results: HP Agile 83% → 97%, Produce Culling 27% → 72%, Expiration 73% → 95%, Shrink ~19% → ~10%.
View details (STAR + LPs)
  • Situation (Dive Deep): Inventory and shrink metrics lagged network peers; data was scattered across reports.
  • Task (Ownership): Act as a program owner for inventory and shrink across all five departments.
  • Actions (Invent & Simplify / Are Right, A Lot): Designed a simple control sheet, defined weekly audit routines, assigned owners, and tied huddle agendas to trends.
  • Results (Deliver Results): Lifted execution metrics into mid/high 90s and cut shrink dollars by more than half over four months, while maintaining $2M+ in monthly shipped volume.
Amazon Fresh · Assistant Manager

Outbound Availability (INF & SDU)

Feb 2025 – Ongoing · Online grocery orders
  • Problem: INF/SDU driven by Ops-controllable causes (>90% of INF) in fresh-heavy categories.
  • Mechanism: Built an Outbound INF/SDU tracker by month, with splits by department, category, and root cause.
  • Execution: Introduced a simple “INF playbook” for pickers (re-check, alternate locations, escalate) and targeted bin checks for high-INF SKUs.
Results: Best-month INF around 4.5% vs peaks >12%; clarified that Last Touch + checkout made up ~61% of INF.
View details (STAR + LPs)
  • Situation (Customer Obsession): Customers saw “item not available” messages even when inventory existed.
  • Task (Bias for Action): Reduce INF/SDU without adding complexity to the picker experience.
  • Actions (Dive Deep): Used INF attribution to focus on Last Touch pick/checkout and false picks; aligned Inbound on stale inventory and inbound delays.
  • Results (Insist on the Highest Standards): Lowered INF in key months and created a repeatable mechanism to keep pressure on root causes.

Paleo Freshness & Shrink Focus

2025 · Produce + Meat & Seafood (Paleo)
  • Problem: High shrink and INF concentrated in Produce, Meat, Dairy, Frozen, Snacks.
  • Mechanism: Used category-level shrink and INF reports to build a “Top Loss Categories” view for Paleo.
  • Execution: Tightened date-audit rotations, culling routines, and display standards; tied coaching to category-level trends.
Results: Shrink dollars decreased while expiration compliance moved from low-70s to mid/high-90s; INF share in key categories was reduced over time.
View details (STAR + LPs)
  • Situation (Dive Deep): Paleo contributed a disproportionate share of shrink and INF vs its sales weight.
  • Task (Ownership): Build a Paleo-specific plan that tied actions directly to shrink and availability metrics.
  • Actions (Invent & Simplify): Created simple category scorecards, rotated tasks to match high-risk days, and assigned clear owners for key displays and cases.
  • Results (Deliver Results): Improved freshness and shrink while improving the customer presentation in high-traffic departments.

Leadership Principles Hall of Fame

Every project mapped to LPs

This portfolio is built around Amazon’s Leadership Principles. Below are examples of how my Amazon Fresh work ties to specific LPs.

Customer Obsession
Protecting the online basket

Outbound INF/SDU work focused on the customer experience of the online order, not just store KPIs: by attacking Ops-controllable INF in Produce/Meat/Dairy, I reduced pain where customers feel it most.

Dive Deep
From “bad metric” to root cause

I used INF attribution (Last Touch pick/checkout, false picks, inbound delays, stale inventory) and shrink buckets (removals, expiration, IA) to pinpoint why metrics were off, not just where.

Deliver Results
Quarter-over-quarter trends

HP Agile 83% → 97%, Produce Culling 27% → 72%, Expiration 73% → 95%, Shrink ~19% → ~10%, and Demand vs Actuals rank moving from low-40s to 32 are all tracked and reported over time, not as one-time wins.

Ownership
Building my own mechanisms

I built and maintain my own Excel control sheets and visuals without being asked, so I can manage my store like a program owner, not just react to ad-hoc asks.

Invent & Simplify
Simple playbooks over complex tools

Instead of asking for new systems, I created simple checklists and “INF playbooks” (re-check, alternate location, escalate) that pickers and leads could apply immediately.

Are Right, A Lot
Letting data challenge assumptions

INF category and attribution data showed where we were actually failing customers (Last Touch, fresh categories), which changed how we prioritized labor and coaching.

Earn Trust
Transparent metrics & follow-through

Sharing visuals (not just numbers) in huddles and updates helps PAs, associates, and leaders see trends clearly and trust that we’re moving in the right direction together.

Learn and Be Curious

Excel + SQL + Python

Examples of how I’d structure the same store data in different tools.

=SUMIFS([INF_Units],[Dept],"Paleo",[Month],$B$1)
/* Highlight Paleo SKUs driving INF in a given month */

=IF([@INF_pct]>0.10,"High","OK")
/* Flag items over 10% INF for coaching */

=AVERAGEIFS([Shrink_Pct],[Quarter],$C$2)
/* Track shrink trends quarter over quarter */
SELECT department,
       SUM(inf_units)   AS inf_units,
       SUM(order_units) AS order_units,
       SUM(inf_units) * 1.0 / NULLIF(SUM(order_units),0) AS inf_pct
FROM   outbound_orders
WHERE  site = 'MAG5'
  AND  order_date BETWEEN '2025-11-01' AND '2025-11-30'
GROUP BY department
ORDER BY inf_pct DESC;

/* Find which departments drive INF% for the month */
import pandas as pd

df = pd.read_csv("inventory_health_mag5.csv")

kpis = (
    df.groupby("quarter")
      [["hp_agile","produce_culling","exp_compliance","shrink_pct"]]
      .mean()
)

high_inf = (
    df[df["inf_pct"] > 0.10]
      .groupby("department")["inf_pct"]
      .mean()
      .sort_values(ascending=False)
)

print(kpis)
print(high_inf)
