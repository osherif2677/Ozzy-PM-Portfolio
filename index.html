Section 2 · Programs & projects

Amazon programs & projects

Translating an Assistant Manager role into program / project work: clear ownership, cross-functional partners, data, and mechanisms.

Inventory Health & Shrink Program

Store-wide program

Built a single program to tackle HP Agile, culling, expiration, cycle counts and shrink across Center Store and Paleo.

Result: HP Agile up 83% → 97%, Produce culling 27% → 72%, expiration compliance 73% → 95%, and shrink reduced from ≈19% → ≈10% of shipped OPS (Jan→Apr).

Star + leadership principles Customer Obsession · Dive Deep · Deliver Results
Situation: Inventory quality issues were driving high shrink and weak compliance scores.
Task: Own a four-wall program to coordinate multiple teams around one set of routines, dashboards and goals.
Actions: Built an Excel control sheet from Inventory Health and shrink reports; ran weekly huddles by department; tied each KPI to concrete tasks in task matrices; coached PAs / associates via one-point lessons.
Results: Significant lifts in compliance and shrink; created reusable mechanisms (dashboards + matrices) that the leadership team still uses to plan labor and audits.

Outbound Availability (INF + SDU)

Customer experience

Owned a structured deep dive into INF and SDU for online grocery orders (Outbound).

Result: best month INF around 4.5% vs peaks near 12–13%; clarified that >90% of INF was Ops-controllable and concentrated in Produce, Dairy, Frozen, Snacks and Meat.

Star + leadership principles Customer Obsession · Dive Deep · Insist on Highest Standards
Situation: Customers were seeing high INF / SDU and substitutions on high-velocity fresh items.
Task: Quantify the drivers, focus on Ops-controllable causes, and build a simple mechanism to keep INF in check.
Actions: Built monthly trackers from INF, SDU and attribution reports; held focused reviews for Paleo and Center Store; tightened bin checks and alternate location checks; connected Outbound misses back to inventory routines and inbound.
Results: INF volatility reduced; teams now see INF / SDU as a shared four-wall problem, not only an Outbound issue.

Demand vs Actuals & Site Rank

Network performance

Connected store-level execution work to the Overall Demand vs Actuals performance the network cares about.

Result: YTD Demand vs Actuals rank improved from low-40s → 32 with a Q4 QTD YoY gain of +381%.

Star + leadership principles Think Big · Are Right, A Lot · Deliver Results

How I work with data

I don’t just read dashboards – I build them. Excel is my primary tool today, with SQL / Python / R growing in. Below are examples of the mechanics I use.

Excel
Store-level KPI control sheet

Monthly tracker combining Inventory Health, INF / SDU, shrink and Demand vs Actuals. Uses pivots, INDEX/MATCH and conditional formatting to highlight risk. 

// Sample structure (one row per month & department)
Month | Dept   | HP_Agile | Produce_Culling | Exp_Compliance
      | INF_Pct | SDU_Pct | Shrink_Pct | Virtual_Gaps | Gaps_With_Stock

=INDEX(InvHealth!C:C,
   MATCH(1,
     (InvHealth!A:A=$A2) * (InvHealth!B:B=$B2),
   0))

// Example: highlight INF > 10% or Shrink > 15%
=IF(OR([@[INF_Pct]]>0.10,[@[Shrink_Pct]]>0.15),"High Risk","OK")
SQL style
INF / SDU aggregation (concept)

How I’d translate my Excel logic into SQL once I have warehouse access. 

-- INF by department & month
SELECT
  DATE_TRUNC('month', order_date)  AS month,
  department,
  SUM(CASE WHEN outcome = 'INF' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::float /
  COUNT(*)                          AS inf_pct,
  SUM(CASE WHEN outcome = 'SDU' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)::float /
  COUNT(*)                          AS sdu_pct
FROM outbound_orders
GROUP BY 1, 2
ORDER BY 1, 2;
