Project 1: Inventory Health Optimization & INF Reduction

Project Overview

This project documents an operational initiative focused on improving inventory health execution to reduce Item Not Found (INF) rates during online order fulfillment.

The goal was not to build a production analytics platform, but to design a lightweight operating mechanism that improves daily execution discipline and produces sustained performance improvement under real operational constraints.

Execution Design
Metrics Ownership
Operational Excellence

Problem Statement

  • INF rates consistently between 7–9%
  • Inconsistent gap scans and go-finds
  • Rolled-over freight creating virtual inventory
  • No standardized daily visibility at sub-department level

Solution Design

ComponentDescription
Daily Data EntryStandardized execution inputs by department and sub-department
Raw Data StoreAppend-only historical tracking for trend analysis
DashboardVisualized INF trends, risk drivers, and inventory health indicators

Dashboard Views

Raw Data Transparency

Results & Impact

  • INF reduced from ~8% → ~4.8–5.0%
  • Execution consistency improved across all departments
  • Rolled freight occurrences decreased
  • Department-level risk drivers became visible and actionable
