About & Career Snapshot

From frontline ops to program-minded leadership

I’m Osman (“Ozzy”) Sherif, an Assistant Manager at Amazon Fresh leading full-store operations and project-style initiatives across Center Store, Kitchen, Outbound, Front End / Customer Experience, and Paleo. In my current role I coordinate daily execution for large associate teams, diagnose operational defects, and drive improvements in inventory integrity, shrink, and online availability.

I earned my B.S. in Psychology from the University of Maryland, supported by data science coursework in R, Python, and research methods. Across these environments, I’ve gravitated toward work that looks and feels like program management: pulling data from multiple systems, turning it into simple control sheets, coordinating stakeholders, and tracking progress over quarters—not just days. I’m building toward Program Manager roles where I can apply this approach at larger scale and through more cross-functional, tech-enabled programs.

Assistant Manager · Amazon Fresh · Chevy Chase, MD

Inventory Health & Shrink Program

Jan 2025 – Ongoing · Center Store, Kitchen, Paleo, Outbound, Front End/CX
  • Situation: Inventory quality KPIs (HP Agile, culling, expiration, gaps) and shrink were below target, causing lost sales, higher waste, and noisy execution.
  • Task: Act as an end-to-end owner for inventory execution and shrink across all five departments, without adding bureaucracy.

  • Results: Raised HP Agile from ~83% to ~97%, produce culling from ~27% to ~72%, and expiration compliance from ~73% to ~95%. Reduced virtual gaps from ~2,000 to ~1,500 per day, gaps-with-stock from ~360 to ~150, and improved shrink from ~19% to ~10% of shipped OPS between January and April.

Assistant Manager · Amazon Fresh · Outbound & Paleo Focus

Outbound Availability (INF & SDU) Initiative

Feb 2025 – Ongoing · Online grocery orders
  • Situation: Online grocery customers experienced INF (Inventory Not Found) and SDU (Same Day Unavailability), with monthly INF ranging from ~4.5% to ~12.5% and early-year SDU around 12%–10% before improving.
  • Task: Build a focused, data-driven approach to lowering INF and SDU—especially in high-impact fresh categories—without overcomplicating workflows.
  • Actions: Created a monthly INF/SDU tracker in Excel segmented by Ops vs non-Ops, category, and INF attribution (e.g., Last Touch OB Pick, Checkout, False Pick Short). Ran deep dives with Paleo and Center Store, tightened bin/location accuracy, and defined a simple “INF playbook” for pickers.
  • Results: Demonstrated best-month INF near 4.5% vs peaks around 12–13%, and gained clear visibility that ~61% of INF came from Last Touch pick/checkout and ~16% from false picks—enabling targeted coaching and process changes.
LPs: Customer Obsession, Dive Deep, Insist on the Highest Standards · PMI Areas: Quality, Risk, Stakeholders

How I use the data

Control Sheets & Decision Support

Each month, I pull key numbers from Inventory Health daily reports, INF and SDU deep dives, shrink reports, and Demand vs Actuals snapshots into my own Excel or Sheets tracker. I use pivot tables and formulas (e.g., IF, SUMIF, INDEX/MATCH) to segment results by category, department, and root cause.

This lets me see trends across quarters, identify which levers are actually moving, and focus huddles and coaching on the highest-impact work. It also helps me communicate clearly with leadership: instead of saying “we’re doing better,” I can show how compliance, gaps, shrink, and site rank are trending together over time.

In other contexts—DSP, IT ops, academic programs—I’ve applied the same mindset, using simple reports and trackers to understand performance, spot patterns, and track follow-through on process changes.
